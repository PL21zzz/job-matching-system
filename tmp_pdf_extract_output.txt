1    EXECUTIVE SUMMARY   In the era of digital transformation, generative artificial intelligence (AI) is  rapidly reshaping professional ecosystems. However, disabled job seekers continue to  face severe technical and structural barriers when navigating legacy recruitment  networks. Manually reviewing complex job descriptions, refining unorganized work  logs, constructing tailored application profiles, and identifying workplace accessibility  facilities consume significant time and effort for these marginalized demographics.   The advancement of large language models offers an unprecedented opportunity  to build inclusive career assistance networks capable of analyzing data profiles,  generating professional assets, and providing barrier-free interactive routing. Driven by  this context, the project “AI-POWERED JOB SEARCH SUPPORT SYSTEM FOR  PEOPLE WITH DISABILITIES” introduces the Equitas AI software platform.   The application enables disabled job seekers to configure private biological  impairment profiles, polish unstructured career records using professional action verbs,  tailor resume metrics against corporate job descriptions, generate context -aware cover  letters, and practice interview reflexes via conversational chatbots and browser -native  voice interfaces. Concurrently, it offers an autonomous accessibility matching engine  intersecting candidate health dynamics with corporate physical workspace facility tags,  alongside centralized portals for human resource personnel. Developed across a secure  web infrastructure using Next.js, NestJS, and Prisma -backed PostgreSQL, the project  delivers a high -performance product with immediate socio -economic utility, ensurin g  employment equity for vulnerable groups and showcasing high commercial viability  within the digital EdTech and HRTech startup landscapes.  The thesis report is structured into four core chapters and a continuous development  framework:  • Chapter 1. Introduction: Presents the foundational motivation for choosing the  topic, system engineering objectives, target research scope, practical  significance, and product scalability boundaries.  • Chapter 2. Literature Review and Theoretical Basis:  Outlines digital  transformation and accessibility frameworks, relevant technology trends,  technical surveys of legacy platforms, market analysis, and architectural  foundational theories.  • Chapter 3. Product Design and Development: Details the system architecture,  relational database schema layout, operational function designs, inclusive user  interface principles, core tech stacks, and the deployment of the MVP version.  • Chapter 4. Implementation and Business Model:  Presents cloud -native  implementation results, functional user scenarios, multi-case test validations, and  proposes the innovative entrepreneurship models and commercialization  roadmaps.
